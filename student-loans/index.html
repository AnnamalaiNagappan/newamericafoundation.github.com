Bipartisan Senate proposal

This proposal emerged as a last-minute solution just days before the scheduled increase in rates. Introduced by Sens. Manchin (D-WV), King (I-ME), Coburn (R-OK), Burr (R-NC), and Alexander (R-TN), it would tie rates to the 10-year Treasury plus 1.85% for all undergraduate Stafford loans. Graduate Stafford loans, however, would be set at the 10-year Treasury rate plus 3.4%. This calculator uses the undergraduate rate. The proposal would save $1 billion over 10 years.

Obama's 2014 budget proposal

President Obama released an interest rate reform proposal in his fiscal year 2014 budget that institutes a market-based rate. The rate would be based on the 10-year Treasury rate plus 0.93% for Subsidized Stafford loans or plus 2.93% for Unsubsidized Stafford loans. Combined with a proposal to expand income-based repayment, it would be deficit neutral over 10 years.

Current law assuming no changes are made

Current law says that on July 1, 2013, rates on Subsidized Stafford loans reset to 6.8%, the same as the rate on Unsubsidized Stafford loans. If Congress fails to act before the start of the 2013-14 school year, rates will remain at 6.8% for both loan types indefinitely. The proposal would have no cost over 10 years because it would not alter the budget baseline.

*Subsidized Stafford loans are available primarily to low-income students and are limited to $3,500 in the first year of school, $4,500 in the second year, and $5,500 thereafter, not to exceed $23,000 total. Unsubsidized Stafford loans are available regardless of financial need and are limited to $9,500 in the first year, $10,500 in the second, and $12,500 thereafter. Total loans may not exceed $57,500, including the $23,000 maximum in Subsidized Stafford loans. For dependent students, total loans may not exceed $31,000. NOTE: This calculator does not account for the minimum monthly payment on federal loans; a minimum amount could make accrued interest and monthly payments lower.
