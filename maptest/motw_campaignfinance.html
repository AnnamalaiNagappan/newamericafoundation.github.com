Even before the candidates hit the campaign trail, pundits have predicted the 2012 election will blow away fundraising and spending records. Now just over a month from the election, where have Obama and Romney invested their money on the ground and where are the campaigns outspending each other? We took a look at expenditure data from the Federal Election Commission to reveal the states where the ratio of spending for each candidate is highest. Both Obama and Romney lead in the states where they headquarter their campaigns, Illinois and Massachusetts respectively. Obama's darling state -- after the District of Columbia -- is the swing state of Wisconsin, where he has over a 50:1 spending advantage over Romney. The state is also host to a direct mail firm Obama relies on to send campaign and fundraising appeals across the country. Romney also lavishes money on key swing states like Virginia, Pennsylvania, and surprisingly New Hampshire, which is host to a GOP-focused direct mail firm responsible for Romney's correspondence nationwide.


The ways big money have affected this election will be up for discussion Tuesday at the New America Foundation for a special campaign event, "Beyond Sticker Shock: How Is Big Money Changing Politics in 2012?" Trevor Potter, the election lawyer who famously enabled Stephen Colbert to start his own Super PAC, will join a discussion on the state of campaign spending in the wake of the Citizens United.
